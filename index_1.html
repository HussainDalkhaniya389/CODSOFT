Our Features

  • Quality

    • The quality that a product has can be a tangible or intangible characteristic that appeals to a specific demographic. Quality may also serve as a point of reference for a product's perceived value. For example, affluent customers may be more likely to buy a brand-name or luxury product that a highly respected company produces instead of a product that a newer company offers.

  • Design

    • Design is a feature category that relates to how a product looks. Improving a product's style or design can help you make it more appealing to customers who are deciding between several companies' offerings. For example, imagine that you work for a company that produces phone cases.

  • Experience

    • Experience is an intangible feature category. This type of feature may not be necessary for a customer's success, but it can help them have more positive involvement with a product and its associated brand. For example, you can consider a company that offers assistance with onboarding for one of its software programs. If a customer receives help with using the program, they may be more likely to view the product and its brand as superior to competitors.
